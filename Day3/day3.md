# Day 3 – Role-Based Prompting

## Topic

Role-Based Prompting

## What I Learned

Role-Based Prompting means assigning Claude a specific persona before asking a question. Instead of receiving generic answers, you receive responses from the perspective of a domain expert.

### Key Concepts

1. Persona-driven responses

   * Different roles generate different styles of thinking and recommendations.

2. Higher quality outputs

   * Role prompts help Claude provide more relevant and specialized answers.

3. Better decision making

   * Problems can be viewed from multiple professional perspectives.

4. Real-world simulation

   * Claude can act as a Founder, Developer, Product Manager, HR Manager, Marketer, Consultant, and more.

---

## Before and After Comparison

### Without Role Prompt

Prompt:
"How do I write better emails?"

Output:
General suggestions about features and improvements.
The response was generic: be clear, concise, and use a good subject line.

### With Role Prompt

Prompt:
"You are a senior executive coach. How do I write better emails?"

Output:
Detailed recommendations regarding user experience, prioritization, retention, and business impact.
The response focused on executive presence, influence, structure, and communication strategy—far more specific and actionable.

### Observation

Role-based prompting produced more specialized and actionable answers than generic prompting.

---

## Key Takeaways

* Assigning a role significantly improves response quality.
* Different personas provide unique perspectives on the same problem.
* Role-based prompting reduces generic responses.
* Expert personas help generate practical and actionable recommendations.
