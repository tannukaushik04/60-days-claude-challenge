The Case For Each Option

Option A: Private Software Development

Strongest case:

Higher earning ceiling, faster skill growth, and it's what your gut already picked — that instinct usually reflects real signal about where your energy naturally goes.

Hidden upside:

Compounding skills in a fast market make you more employable elsewhere too — private-sector experience is portable in a way government tenure often isn't.

Biggest weakness:

Directly feeds your named fears — layoffs and burnout are real, structural risks in this sector, not just anxiety.

Best if you value:

Growth, ceiling, and are willing to actively manage risk instead of avoid it.

Option B: Government IT Job

Strongest case:

Directly matches your stated goal — high stability, low layoff risk, predictable hours that guard against burnout.

Hidden upside:

Stability isn't just comfort — it's a stable base that can fund side projects, upskilling, or a later private-sector move on your own terms, not out of desperation.

Biggest weakness:

Slower growth and lower ceiling could leave you feeling stuck or under-challenged — the exact restlessness your gut may already sense.

Best if you value:

Predictability, lower stress, and a longer time horizon over speed.

Assumption Buster

Assumptions you may be making

  • That private sector = certain burnout and certain layoffs, rather than a variable risk you can manage or mitigate.
  • That government = permanently safe, when IT roles there still face restructuring, budget cuts, and hiring freezes.
  • That "stability" and "growth" are mutually exclusive, rather than sequential — you can pick one first and move later.

Biases at play

  • Loss aversion — the fear of losing stability looms larger than the appeal of the gains you'd get from growth.
  • Status quo bias — "safe" options feel inherently correct just because they resist change, not because you've verified they fit your goal.

What you're definitely ignoring

You haven't mentioned a financial runway or savings buffer — that number, not your gut feeling, should be doing a lot of the deciding here.
