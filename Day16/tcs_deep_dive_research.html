Business overview
TCS is India's largest IT services company and the second-largest by market cap on NSE. It provides IT services, consulting, and business solutions to clients across BFSI, manufacturing, retail, telecom, and government sectors in 46+ countries. With $30 billion+ in annual USD revenue, it crossed a historic milestone in FY25. The company is pivoting aggressively toward AI — annualised AI revenues reached $2.3 billion in Q4 FY26, with its Hypervault AI infrastructure business signing partnerships with OpenAI, AMD, and ABB.

Valuation verdict
At ~16× trailing P/E, TCS is trading near its lowest multiple in a decade — well below its 5-year average of 28–32×. This re-rating reflects two concerns: (1) macro uncertainty suppressing discretionary IT spending, and (2) "AI deflation" — the risk that AI tools reduce billable hours for traditional IT work. However, the dividend yield of ~5% and strong FCF generation provide a meaningful floor. Versus peers, TCS is in line with Infosys (P/E ~16–17×) but cheaper than HCL (~18–20×). Interpretation: Fair-to-cheap vs history; fair vs current peers

Growth assessment
Revenue growth has moderated from the post-COVID boom (18–20% in FY22–23) to mid-single digits in FY26. In constant currency terms, FY26 revenue actually declined 2.4%, highlighting the challenge of rupee depreciation masking real weakness. However, Q4FY26 marked the third consecutive quarter of sequential growth — a positive trend reversal. The order book at $40.7B (FY26 TCV) provides revenue visibility. The AI revenue ramp-up ($2.3B annualised) is a key growth driver to watch. Trend: Slowing but potentially bottoming

Financial health assessment
TCS runs an exceptionally clean balance sheet — virtually zero long-term debt, which means no interest risk even in a rising-rate environment. Its cash conversion (over 100% of net profit) is best-in-class. The company returned nearly ₹45,000 Cr to shareholders through dividends in FY25 alone — a remarkable shareholder-return policy. Operating margins have rebounded to a 4-year high, aided by wage-increase timing and operational leverage. Overall health: Strong

Shareholding breakdown
Promoters 71.77%, DII 13.41%, FII 9.66%, Public 4.52%, Others 0.64%.
Tata Sons holds ~72% — one of the highest promoter stakes among Indian large-caps. This concentration provides governance stability but means the free float is small (~28%), which can amplify price moves. Zero pledging is a strong positive signal. FII holding at 9.66% is moderate; a potential re-rating could attract foreign flows back.
Strengths (moat & fundamentals)
Unmatched scale & client stickiness — 600K+ employees, 46 countries, decade-long relationships with global banks and Fortune 500 firms. Switching costs are very high in large IT engagements.
AI revenue ramp — Annualised AI revenue hit $2.3B in Q4FY26, growing rapidly. Hypervault partnerships (OpenAI, AMD) position TCS as infrastructure layer for enterprise AI — not just a service layer.
Pristine balance sheet & FCF machine — Zero debt, FCF exceeding net profit, ₹110/share dividend in FY26. Dividend yield ~5% at CMP provides income cushion.
Margin recovery — FY26 operating margin of 25% is a 4-year high. Indicates cost discipline improving even as the company invests in AI capability.
Order book visibility — $40.7B TCV in FY26, including 5 mega deals and 3 in Q4 alone. Provides 15–18 months of revenue pipeline comfort.
Watch-points (risks)
AI deflation risk — CEO Krithivasan acknowledged "degrowth" — AI tools reducing billable hours per project. This structural risk is industry-wide but could disproportionately impact scale players like TCS.
USD revenue growth stalled — FY26 constant-currency revenue fell 2.4%. Most clients are US-domiciled; macro slowdown in BFSI and discretionary tech spending is a headwind.
Legal exposure — US Supreme Court rejected TCS's appeal in the DXC Technology trade secrets case. Total provisioning is $220M, with $70M still to be charged — an earnings risk in FY27.
Stock underperformance — TCS has underperformed Nifty50 by ~30 percentage points over 1 year, and mid-cap IT peers significantly outpaced it. Multiple re-rating requires visible USD growth recovery.
Key metric to track
📍 Constant-currency revenue growth — this is the single metric that will drive a re-rating. If TCS can show 4–6% CC growth for 2 consecutive quarters, expect multiple expansion. Watch each earnings call for CC revenue trajectory and BFSI recovery signals.

📍 AI revenue monetisation — $2.3B annualised is impressive but watch if it cannibalises traditional revenue or adds to it. TCV from AI-native deals is the leading indicator.
