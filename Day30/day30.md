# Day 30 – Supply Chain Builder

## Challenge
Build a **Supply Chain Builder** using a single HTML file with React (CDN), JavaScript, HTML, and CSS.

The simulator teaches beginners how supply chains work by allowing users to design and optimize a company's supply chain while balancing business trade-offs such as cost, speed, sustainability, customer satisfaction, and operational risk.

---

# Project Overview

The application simulates the role of a **Supply Chain Operations Manager**.

Users are introduced to a randomly generated company and make a series of strategic supply chain decisions.

Each decision immediately updates important business metrics, allowing users to understand the impact of every operational choice.

The simulation concludes with a complete enterprise dashboard showing overall performance, strengths, weaknesses, risks, and recommended improvements.

- Beginner-friendly supply chain introduction

- Dark premium dashboard

# Supply Chain Decisions

The simulator guides users through:

1. Supplier Strategy
   - Single Supplier
   - Multiple Suppliers

2. Factory Location
   - Nearshore Factory
   - Offshore Factory
   - Regional Factory

3. Warehouse Strategy
   - Central Warehouse
   - Regional Warehouses
   - Distributed Warehouses

4. Transportation
   - Road
   - Rail
   - Sea
   - Air

5. Inventory Strategy
   - Low Inventory
   - Balanced Safety Stock
   - High Inventory

Each decision explains:

- What it means
- Why it matters
- Advantages
- Disadvantages
- Business impact

# Key Learnings

During this project I learned:

- How supply chains connect sourcing, production, warehousing, transportation, and inventory.
- Why business decisions involve trade-offs rather than perfect solutions.
- How supplier diversification reduces operational risk.
- How transportation choices affect cost, speed, and sustainability.
- Why balanced inventory improves resilience without excessive holding costs.
- How React state can dynamically update business metrics.
- How enterprise dashboards visualize operational performance.
- How random simulations create engaging educational applications.
